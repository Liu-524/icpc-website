What?

Illinois Programming League (IPL) is a series of algorithmic programming contests organized by ACM SIG-ICPC at UIUC. Contestants solve problems that are likely to be asked in technical interviews. Come in practice and sharpen your programming skills!

When?

Every Monday, 7-9 PM
Starting from 3rd week of school

Where?

Siebel 2405

Food and T-shirt

Free Pizza will be provided for IPL contestants!
Each contestant who competes in at least four contests will receive a free IPL t-shirt.

Eligibility

Anyone is allowed to participate, get free dinner and t-shirt.

You are eligible for prizes if you are
  • Enrolled in University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, and
  • Started college in 2014 or later OR born in 1995 or later.

Rules

Students compete individually and communication during the contest is not allowed.

Bring your own laptop and setup the programming environment in your favorite language.

During the contest internet usage (Google, Stack Overflow, etc) is forbidden, with the following exceptions:
  • Beginner's Guide
  • Beginner's Cheatsheet
  • Time Complexity Cheatsheet
  • C++ 11 Reference
  • Java 8 Reference
  • Python 3 Reference

Any physical reference materials such as notes, textbooks, printed code, etc are allowed. Electronic and digital reference materials are forbidden.

Scoring:
  • Each problem is worth the same.
  • Total time = the sum of (time since the begining) for each problem solved.
  • The contestants will be ranked by the most problems solved. If there is a tie, they will be ranked by the least total time.
  • There is a 20-minute penalty added to the total time for each wrong submission.

The rules are tentative and are subjective to change at our discretion.

Languages allowed

  • C++ 11
  • Java 8
  • Python 3
