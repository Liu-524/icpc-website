What?

The Illinois Programming League (IPL), a series of ICPC-style programming contests open to all UIUC students. Keep excelling in the contests and claim the rewards!

Don't forget to fill up our registration form! You can do it anytime in the semester.

When and Where?

Every Monday, 7-9 PM, Room 0218 (basement), Siebel Center

Why?

It's fun! The IPL is a perfect opportunity for people who enjoy solving challenging algorithmic programming problems. Competing in the IPL will greatly boost your skills and help you ace in classes and interviews. Also get ready to meet some of the greatest programmers in the university! (Plus, food every week and prizes over $1000!)

Rules

  • Students compete individually and communication during the contest is not allowed.
  • Bring your own laptop or use the EWS machines.
  • During the contest, one should not access the internet, with the following exceptions:
    • Language references
  • One can bring reference materials of any number of pages. Currently we allow the use of pdf or other electronic formats, to make everyone's life easier. However, copy and paste from any source is strictly prohibited. In other words, every character you submit must be typed by hand during the competition. No template files can be used, unless you type them again.
  • Scoring:
    • Each problem is worth the same.
    • Total time = the sum of (time since the begining) for each problem solved
    • The contestants will be ranked by the most problems solved. If there is a tie, they will be ranked by the least total time.
    • There is a 20-minute penalty added to the total time for each wrong submission.
  • The rules are tentative and are subjective to change at our discretion.

Languages allowed

  • C
  • C++
  • Java
  • Python

The versions are dependent on which specific problems we use. However we will try to find problems that supports the relatively new standards (C99, C++11, Java 8, Python 3).
